# EU261 — Complete Rules Reference

Full legal text: Regulation (EC) No 261/2004
Source: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2004/261/oj

## Scope (Article 3)
Applies to:
- (a) Passengers departing from an airport located in the territory of an EU Member State
- (b) Passengers departing from a third-country airport to an EU airport, IF the operating airline is an EU (Community) carrier

Also covers: Iceland, Norway, Switzerland (via bilateral agreements)
Does NOT apply to passengers who travel free or at a reduced fare not available to the public.

## When compensation is due (Article 7)

### Delay (Case C-402/07 Sturgeon, confirmed by CJEU)
- Passenger arrives at final destination **3 or more hours late** = treated like cancellation for compensation purposes
- Measure arrival delay at FINAL destination, not first stop

### Cancellation (Article 5)
Eligible UNLESS the airline notified you:
- At least **14 days** before scheduled departure, OR
- 7–13 days before AND offered re-routing arriving ≤ 2h late and departing ≤ 2h early, OR
- < 7 days before AND offered re-routing arriving ≤ 1h late and departing ≤ 1h early

### Denied Boarding (Article 4)
Always eligible (unless passenger voluntarily gave up seat).

## Compensation amounts (Article 7)

| Flight distance | Amount |
|---|---|
| ≤ 1,500 km | **€250** |
| Intra-EU > 1,500 km, OR any flight 1,500–3,500 km | **€400** |
| All other flights > 3,500 km | **€600** |

Distance = great-circle distance between departure and final destination.

### 50% reduction (Article 7(2))
If airline offers re-routing arriving within:
- 2 hours of original (flights ≤ 1500 km) → €125
- 3 hours of original (flights 1500–3500 km) → €200
- 4 hours of original (flights > 3500 km) → €300

## Extraordinary Circumstances (Article 5(3))
Airline NOT liable if cause is "extraordinary circumstances that could not have been avoided even if all reasonable measures had been taken."

### ✅ Counts as extraordinary (airline wins):
- Severe weather (thunderstorm, volcanic ash, fog beyond limits)
- Air Traffic Control strike
- Airport closure by authorities
- Bird strike (Case C-315/15 Pešková)
- Sabotage, terrorism, political instability
- Hidden manufacturing defect revealed by manufacturer

### ❌ Does NOT count (passenger wins):
- Technical/mechanical problems with the aircraft (Case C-549/07 Wallentin-Hermann)
- Crew running out of legal duty hours
- Airline staff strike (Case C-195/17 Krüsemann) — own staff strike is NOT extraordinary
- Operational issues (late arrival of previous leg)

## Right to care (Article 9) — applies on ANY long delay (regardless of compensation)
- Meals and refreshments (after 2h delay for ≤1500km, 3h for 1500–3500km, 4h for >3500km)
- Two free phone calls / emails
- Hotel + transfers if overnight stay needed
- These apply EVEN IF airline can use extraordinary-circumstances defense!
