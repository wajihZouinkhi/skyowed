# USA Passenger Rights

## Domestic flights (within USA)
- **No compensation by law for delays or cancellations.**
- Airlines must follow their own "Customer Service Plan" (self-written).

## Bumping / Oversold flights (14 CFR Part 250)
| Arrival delay vs original | Domestic | International |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1 hour | $0 | $0 |
| 1–2 hours | 200% of fare (max $1,075) | — |
| 1–4 hours | — | 200% of fare (max $1,075) |
| > 2 hours (domestic) / > 4 hours (intl) | 400% of fare (max $2,150) | 400% of fare (max $2,150) |

## DOT 2024 Refund Rule
Airlines MUST automatically refund passengers (no need to ask) when:
- Domestic flight delayed > 3 hours OR international > 6 hours, AND passenger chooses not to travel
- Cancelled flight and passenger chooses not to travel
- Significant schedule change (> 3h domestic / > 6h intl)

## International flights — Montreal Convention Article 19
For flights between MC-1999 signatory countries:
- Airline liable for damages caused by delay (expenses: hotel, meals, missed connections)
- Up to ~ 5,346 SDR (~$7,100)
- Requires proof of actual expenses — NOT automatic compensation
